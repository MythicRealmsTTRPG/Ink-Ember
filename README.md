# Ink & Ember

**Ink & Ember** is a worldbuilding and narrative management platform for writers and tabletop creators who work on long-term, complex projects.

It keeps the familiar **wiki-style reading and navigation** of tools like World Anvil, while focusing on **structured data, creator ownership, and scalability** for worlds that grow over years instead of collapsing under their own lore.

Ink & Ember is designed for people who are not “done worldbuilding” — and never plan to be.

---

## Philosophy

Ink & Ember is built on a few simple principles:

- **Wiki-first presentation**  
  Lore should be readable, browsable, and linkable like a traditional wiki.

- **Structured under the hood**  
  Articles are not just pages — they are typed, field-driven entries that can be queried, related, and reused across timelines, campaigns, and projects.

- **Creator ownership**  
  Your work should not be trapped in a platform. Ink & Ember is designed with exportability and long-term access in mind.

- **Scales with complexity**  
  Whether you are running a single tabletop campaign or managing a shared universe with decades of history, Ink & Ember is built to grow with you.

## Core Features

### Wiki-Style Articles
- Familiar article pages with sections, links, and infoboxes
- Typed article categories (Characters, Locations, Cultures, etc.)
- Optional structured fields without forcing rigid templates
- Cross-linking between all content

### Timelines & Calendars
- Multiple calendars per world
- Events that can appear in multiple timelines
- Articles can reference events directly
- Chronicles act as curated narrative views of timelines

### Chronicles
- Campaign logs, story arcs, historical records
- Built on top of articles and events (not isolated text blobs)
- Ideal for tabletop sessions, novels, or shared canon tracking

### Variables
- World states (pre-war / post-war)
- Canon layers and campaign branches
- Visibility controls (public knowledge, secrets, spoilers)
- Supports “what-if” and alternate timelines

### Maps
- Link regions, settlements, and routes directly to articles
- Designed to support narrative context, not just geography

### Relationships & Power Structures
- Family trees for characters
- Diplomatic relations between organizations or nations
- Structured representation of alliances, conflicts, and hierarchies

### Creator Tools
- Notebooks and whiteboards for unstructured planning
- To-do tracking for long-term projects
- Manuscripts for long-form prose (optional, non-invasive)

## Foundry VTT Integration (Planned)

Ink & Ember is being designed with **Foundry Virtual Tabletop** integration in mind.

The goal is to allow creators to move cleanly between **worldbuilding canon** and **tabletop play** without duplicating work:

- Link Ink & Ember articles to Foundry entities (Actors, Items, Journals, Scenes, Roll Tables)
- Export structured data for use in Foundry compendiums and handouts
- Publish or sync selected lore based on visibility rules (public knowledge, secrets, spoilers)
- Support campaign logs and chronicles that reference Foundry sessions and outcomes

This integration will be **optional**. Ink & Ember remains fully usable as a standalone worldbuilding platform.

- Natural Law
- Material
- Technology
- Species
- Culture / Ethnicity
- Language
- Religion
- Gods / Deities
- Myth
- Tradition

### Civilization & Power
- Country
- Settlement
- Organization
- Military
- Military Formation
- Profession
- Title
- Diplomatic Relations

### Characters & Actors
- Character
- Condition
- Conflict
- Family Trees (relationship views)

### Narrative & Canon
- Plot
- Prose
- Chronicle
- Session Report
- Document
- Manuscript

### Items & Systems
- Item
- Spell
- Vehicle

### Meta & Utility
- Generic
- Variables

Article categories are configurable and extensible. Users are not forced to use every type.

## What Ink & Ember Is Not

- Not a social network
- Not a marketplace
- Not a gamified lore leaderboard
- Not designed around short-term content churn

Ink & Ember is for **projects measured in years**, not sessions.

---

## Intended Audience

- Tabletop Game Masters running long campaigns
- Writers working on series, sagas, or shared universes
- Worldbuilders maintaining large, interconnected settings
- Creators who value structure without sacrificing readability
